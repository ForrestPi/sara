std::vector<int> DO::Sara::find_peaks ( const Array< T, N, 1 > &  orientation_histogram,
T  peak_ratio_thres = T(0.8) 
)

This is used in [Lowe, IJCV 2004] to find histogram peaks.

A histogram peak is by definition the index \(i\) such that:

  • \(h_{i} > h_{i+1}\)
  • \(h_{i} > h_{i-1}\)

Only histogram peaks \(i\) such that \(h_i \geq 0.8 \max_j h_j\)

Image<Matrix<T,2,1> > DO::Sara::gradient_polar_coordinates ( const Image< T > &  f)

Computes the image gradients of image \(I\) in polar coordinates.

In each pixel \((x,y) \in [0,w[ \times [0,h[\), \(\nabla I(x,y)\) is the 2D vector \((r,\theta)\) where:

  • \(r = 2 ||\nabla I(x,y)||\),
  • \(\theta = \mathrm{angle}( \nabla I(x,y) )\).
void DO::Sara::lowe_smooth_histogram ( Array< T, N, 1 > &  orientation_histogram)

This is used in [Lowe, IJCV 2004] to determine keypoint orientations.

Basically, the histogram is convolved 6 times with the mean kernel \([1/3, 1/3, 1/3]\).
